Structure as Program: Studio Font Redefines the Cliff House with Ten Inhabitable Columns
Dezeen · Jul 16 · Cross-verified: Dezeen · Studio Font

Studio Font's Casa Diez Pies (Ten-Foot House) perches on a Pacific cliff in Mazunte, Mexico. The 250m² home is raised on 10 large concrete columns that house staircases, bathrooms, and storage — achieving what founder Raquel Font calls "structure, programme, and enclosure simultaneously."

Design Concept: The columns' curved geometry was inspired by the eroded rock formations of nearby Punta Cometa. The floor slabs step down the hillside following natural topography, creating a sequence of interconnected living spaces with continuous Pacific views.

Materials: Warm terracotta-pigmented cast-in-situ concrete, manually bent reinforcement bars with custom formwork for curved corners. Timber wall linings and bespoke carpentry contrast with the concrete. Some column tops are capped with glass to create skylights; others feature small planters.

A Tale of Two Cities: Delhi and Mumbai Chart Divergent Heritage Renewal Paths
New Indian Express · Time Out · Hindustan Times · Free Press Journal · Jul 10–15 · Cross-verified: 4 sources

Delhi — Chandni Chowk (top-down, government-led): The 376-year-old marketplace designed by Jahanara Begum in 1650 stretches 1,400 yards from Red Fort to Fatehpuri Mosque. The Delhi government has renamed Shahjahanabad Redevelopment Corporation as Indraprastha Virasat Punarvikas Nigam, prioritizing Chandni Chowk's redevelopment. The 7.12km² Old City contains one UNESCO World Heritage Site, 10 nationally protected monuments, and 700+ locally protected heritage sites.

Mumbai — Kala Ghoda (bottom-up, spatial activation): BMC's ₹8 crore "Art Avenue" project transformed 3,443m² across five heritage streets with grey/black granite and basalt paving. Public art, murals, and sculptures by JJ School of Arts students accompany al fresco dining plans and vehicle-free weekends. CM Fadnavis declared "Mumbai has gained a new artistic segment."

Nicosia Launches €4.1M Competition for Historic Municipal Garden
Cyprus Mail · Jul 17
Designed by modernist architect Neoptolemos Michaelides, the listed garden faces sensitive restoration. The mayor mandates "every intervention must respect historical character and, where required, be reversible." 22,500+ trees planted citywide in two years.

Three 3D-Printing Breakthroughs: MIT Quantifies Hardware Limits, Ireland Goes Cement-Free, Seaweed Strengthens Earth
MIT News · Phys.org · Additive Manufacturing · Industry Tap · Jul 8–16 · Cross-verified: 4 sources

MIT — Quantifying the Hardware Bottleneck. The mixed-integer optimization framework bakes three printer constraints — bead width, turning radius, single continuous path — directly into topology optimization. The 2.3m bridge revealed that below 200,000 lbs of load, design is entirely driven by "can I build it?" rather than physics. Shrinking bead width from 4cm to 1cm could save 76% material. Next: integrating rebar into printed structures.

Trinity College Dublin — Cement-Free Geopolymer. Ireland's first demonstration of a 3D-printed geopolymer using bauxite refining residue. Over 30% of binder content comes from local industrial waste. Preliminary assessment: ~70% reduction in embodied carbon vs. conventional Portland cement concrete.

Seaweed Extract for Earth-Based Printing. 0.12% sodium alginate boosted compressive strength by 25% and print speed by 33% in clay-sand mixtures. A 60-degree overhang was printed without collapse, pointing toward on-site reuse of excavated soil.

U. Stuttgart: Biocatalytic Fabrication with Enzyme-Activated Chitosan Hydrogel
Construction Robotics (Springer) · Jul 8
First architectural-scale demonstrator integrating biocatalysis into additive manufacturing. Enzyme-activated chitosan composite achieves 2m+ span thin-shell structures under ambient conditions, with material differentiation through toolpath strategy rather than multi-cartridge extrusion.
Cellulose Nanofibers Enable 40% Cement Reduction in 3D-Printed Concrete
Nature Communications · 2026
0.3% CNF + 29% limestone filler: static yield stress up 1213%, 34.4% lower GWP, 12.1% lower cost. Robotic-arm printing validated large-scale overhang geometries. CNF identified as the most cost-efficient additive for enhancing static yield stress.
