Structure as Program: Studio Font's Ten Inhabitable Columns Redefine the Cliff House
Dezeen · Jul 16 · Cross-verified: Dezeen · Studio Font

Studio Font's Casa Diez Pies (Ten-Foot House) perches on a Pacific cliff in Mazunte, Mexico. The 250m² home is raised on 10 large concrete columns that house staircases, bathrooms, and storage — achieving "structure, programme, and enclosure simultaneously," founder Raquel Font told Dezeen. The curved column geometry was inspired by the eroded rock formations of nearby Punta Cometa.

Spatial strategy: Floor slabs step down following natural topography, creating a sequence of interconnected living spaces with continuous Pacific views. Full-height folding wooden shutters extend interiors onto individual balconies.

Materials: Warm terracotta-pigmented cast-in-situ concrete with manually bent rebar and custom formwork for curved corners. Timber linings contrast with the concrete. Some column tops capped with glass as skylights, others with planters.

Three Paths for Heritage Urban Renewal: Delhi, Rio, and Nicosia
New Indian Express · prefeitura.rio · Cyprus Mail · Times of India · Jul 9–17 · Cross-verified: 4 sources

Delhi — Chandni Chowk (top-down government renewal): The Delhi government renamed Shahjahanabad Redevelopment Corporation as IVPN, prioritizing Chandni Chowk's redevelopment. The 7.12km² Old City contains one World Heritage Site, 10 nationally + 10 state-protected monuments, and 700+ locally listed properties. But conservation architect Anisha Shekhar Mukherji calls for "people-first," incremental interventions. Historian Swapna Liddle argues: "redevelopment is misguided — Old Delhi is a living historic place, not a monument."

Rio — Praça Onze (public-private urban renewal): Complementary Law No. 301 of July 9 creates a 458,000m² Special Urban Interest Area with R$1.7 billion over 20 years and 37,000 planned residential units. The architectural reference is Francis Kéré's "Library of Knowledge." The 31 de Março Elevated Highway will be demolished for the Democracy Avenue. Funded by municipal property sales, not Cepacs.

Nicosia — Municipal Garden (competition-driven): A €4.1M architectural competition to restore the listed garden designed by modernist architect Neoptolemos Michaelides. Mayor Charalambos Prountzos: "Every intervention must respect historical and architectural character and, where required, be reversible." 22,500+ trees planted citywide in two years.

Bottlenecks and Breakthroughs: MIT Quantifies Hardware Limits, Ireland Goes Cement-Free, Seaweed Strengthens Earth
MIT News · Additive Manufacturing · Phys.org · Industry Tap · 3DPI · Jul 8–17 · Cross-verified: 5 sources

MIT — Quantifying the Hardware Bottleneck. The Additive Manufacturing paper embeds three printer constraints — bead width, turning radius, single continuous path — directly into topology optimization. The 2.3m bridge used off-the-shelf mortar, printed in ~30 min, and held 2,000+ lbs without measurable bending. Key insight: below 200,000 lbs, design is entirely "can I build it?" driven. Bead width is the biggest lever — 1cm vs. 4cm saves 76% material. Prof. Carstensen: "I thought the continuous path would be the problem, but it was the bead width." Next: integrating rebar.

Trinity College Dublin — Cement-Free Geopolymer. Ireland's first 3D-printed geopolymer uses bauxite refining residue. No Portland cement; 30%+ binder from local industrial waste. Embodied carbon ~70% below conventional concrete. Next steps: mechanical performance, durability, reinforcement, regulatory compliance.

Seaweed Extract — 0.12% Additive, 25% Stronger. Sodium alginate from brown seaweed changes clay particle interactions, enabling smoother flow through 3D printers and near-instant stabilization. 33% faster printing, 60° overhang printed without collapse. Could enable on-site reuse of excavated soil as structural walls.

U. Stuttgart: Biocatalytic Fabrication with Enzyme-Activated Chitosan Hydrogel
Construction Robotics (Springer) · Jul 8
First architectural-scale demonstrator integrating biocatalysis into additive manufacturing. 2m+ span thin-shell structure under ambient conditions. Material differentiation through toolpath strategy, not multi-cartridge extrusion.
Cellulose Nanofibers Enable 40% Cement Reduction in 3D-Printed Concrete
Nature Communications · 2026
0.3% CNF + 29% limestone filler: 1213% increase in static yield stress, 34.4% lower GWP, 12.1% lower minimum selling price. CNF identified as most cost-efficient additive for static yield stress enhancement.
